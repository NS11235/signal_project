As per the requirements,
we made three subclasses that inherit from an abstract class DataListener, 
we decided that a boolean isConnected, a connect method, 
a disconnect method, 
and a listen method were what all the subclasses would have in common. 
From there, we decided that the differences lied in the fields, 
while the methods overrid the abstract class’s methods. 
This allowed us to rely on the common methods to get the information that we need, 
without having to specifically check what kind of listener we were using. 
The information received from the Listeners would be used by the DataSourceAdapter which would listen on any information received and store it in a field, 
to be parsed by the DataParser, 
this chain of information of passing from one object to the next was chosen to abide by the single responsibility principle, 
allowing the DataSourceAdapter to take the responsibility of storing the data and preparing it for the parser rather than the parser immediately parsing data as received.
